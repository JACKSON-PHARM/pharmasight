# PharmaSight YouTube Channel – Content Roadmap

A practical roadmap for launching a YouTube channel that teaches users how to use PharmaSight (pharmacy management for retail pharmacies in Kenya).

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–2)

### 1.1 Channel setup
- [ ] **Create the channel** – Name ideas: “PharmaSight Tutorials”, “PharmaSight Kenya”, “Learn PharmaSight”
- [ ] **Branding** – Use PharmaSight logo/colors (teal #14b8a6, dark #0f172a). Banner + profile pic.
- [ ] **About & links** – Short description, link to app/demo, optional website or support contact.
- [ ] **Playlists** – Create empty playlists now; add videos as you publish (see Section 4).

### 1.2 Production basics
- [ ] **Recording** – OBS (free) or similar for screen + optional webcam.
- [ ] **Microphone** – Any clear mic; avoid heavy echo (quiet room or basic treatment).
- [ ] **Resolution** – 1080p minimum; 1280×720 or 1920×1080 for browser window.
- [ ] **Length** – Aim 5–15 minutes per tutorial; “quick tips” can be 2–4 minutes.

### 1.3 First “trailer” video (optional but recommended)
- **Title:** “What is PharmaSight? | Pharmacy Management for Kenya”
- **Content:** 2–3 min overview: who it’s for (retail pharmacies in Kenya), main benefits (inventory, POS, KRA compliance, FEFO, multi-branch), and what the series will cover.
- **Goal:** First-time visitors understand the product and what they’ll learn.

---

## Phase 2: Core Tutorial Series (Weeks 3–8)

Order topics to match real user flow: setup → items → stock → purchases → sales → reports.

| # | Video topic | Description | Est. length |
|---|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Getting started & first login** | Create account / invite, log in, choose branch, tour of sidebar (Landing, Dashboard, Sales, Purchases, Inventory, Expenses, Reports, Settings). | 8–12 min |
| 2 | **Settings that matter** | Company/branch, VAT, user, API (if self-hosted). What to set before daily use. | 6–10 min |
| 3 | **Adding your first items** | Items page: add product, name, SKU, unit (tablets/ml etc.), pack size, breaking bulk, cost/selling price. | 10–14 min |
| 4 | **Understanding breaking bulk** | Why base units, pack vs piece, how it affects POS and stock. Quick demo. | 5–8 min |
| 5 | **Receiving stock (Purchases / GRN)** | Create GRN, add items, batch number, expiry date, quantity, FEFO. How stock updates. | 10–12 min |
| 6 | **Selling at the counter (POS)** | Sales page: search item, add to cart, quantity, price/margin, FEFO allocation. Complete sale, print receipt. | 10–14 min |
| 7 | **Checking inventory** | Inventory: current stock, by batch, expiry view. Search and filters. When to reorder. | 6–10 min |
| 8 | **Invoices & KRA compliance** | How invoices are numbered, VAT, credit notes. What to keep for KRA. | 6–9 min |
| 9 | **Expenses** | Record expenses, link to branch/period. Why it matters for reporting. | 5–8 min |
| 10 | **Reports you’ll use** | Dashboard + Reports: sales summary, stock value, expiring items, batch report. How to use them daily. | 8–12 min |

**Publishing:** 1–2 videos per week. Pin a “Start here” comment or use a “Getting started” playlist.

## Phase 3: Deep dives & problem-solving (Ongoing)

After the core series, add videos that answer “how do I…?” and “why?”.

### Inventory & stock
- Stock take: full process, reconciling differences.
- Manual adjustments: when and how (damaged, found, corrections).
- Batch tracking and FEFO: what it does and how it shows in POS/Inventory.
- Expiry alerts and using “expiring soon” in decisions.

### Sales & POS
- Returns and credit notes (step-by-step).
- Multiple units in one sale (e.g. 2 packs + 3 loose).
- Receipts and printing (thermal vs A4 if both exist).

### Purchases & suppliers
- GRN with multiple batches.
- Editing or cancelling a GRN (if supported).
- Using procurement recommendations (if the app suggests what to order).

### Multi-branch & admin
- Switching branches.
- Branch-level reports vs company-wide (if applicable).
- User roles and invites (if you add this later).

### Compliance & best practices
- KRA: invoice numbering, VAT, keeping records.
- Audit trail: who did what and when.
- Backups and data (general advice, not app-specific if not in scope).

## Phase 5: Consistency & growth

### Upload schedule
- **Minimum:** 1 video every 2 weeks.
- **Target:** 1 video per week during the core series, then 1 every 1–2 weeks.

### Titles & thumbnails
- **Titles:** Include “PharmaSight” + clear action: “How to add items in PharmaSight”, “POS sale in PharmaSight”.
- **Thumbnails:** Same style (logo, colors), clear text (e.g. “Add Items”, “POS Sale”), face optional.

### Descriptions
- First 2 lines: what the viewer will learn (important for search).
- Timestamps for long videos (e.g. 0:00 Intro, 1:20 Adding item, 4:00 Breaking bulk).
- Link to app/demo/support in every description.

### Where to share
- Pharmacy / pharma groups (Kenya-focused Facebook, WhatsApp, LinkedIn).
- Your own site or landing page.
- Email to existing or trial users.
- Partner with pharmacy associations or training centers if relevant.

### Collecting ideas
- Keep a list of questions from users (support, comments, WhatsApp).
- Turn each recurring question into a short video or a section in a longer one.
